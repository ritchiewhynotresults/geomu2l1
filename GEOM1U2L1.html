Postulate

Something that is accepted to be true.
Example: geometric definitions and vocabulary.

Theorem

Something that has to be proved, using postulates.

Postulates that deal with points, lines and planes.

1. Though any two points, there is exactly one line.

2. Through any three points not at the same line, there is exactly one plane.

3. A line contains at least two points.

4. A plane contains at least three points not on the same line.

5. If two points line in a plane, then the entire line containing those points lies in that plane.

6. If two lines intersect, then their intersection is exactly one point.

7. If two planes intersect, then their intersection is a line.

Postulates that deal with segments

8. Ruler postulate:

Given any two points A and B on a line, A corresponds to zero and B to a positive real number.

That means that, if we take number line, and we place point A at 8 and point B at 15, point A corresponds to zero, and we count the units from 8 to 15, starting at 0 (point A), giving us as a result the distance between these points, which would be 7.

9. Segment addition postulate:

If B is between A and C, then AB + BC = AC.

That means that the sum of the two portions of the segments results in the entire segment

THEOREMS

1. Midpoint theorem:

If M is the midpoint of segment AB, then segment AM is congruent to segment MB.

2. Segment congruence theorem says that if we have a segment AB, the following properties may be present:

  • Reflexive: AB ≅ AB

    (segment AB is congruent to itself)

  • Symmetric:If AB ≅ CD, then CD ≅ AB

    (If segement AB is congruent to segment CD, then segment CD is congruent to segment AB)

  • Transitive:If AB ≅ CD, and CD ≅ EF, then AB ≅ EF

    (If segement AB is congruent to segment CD, then segment CD is congruent to segment EF, then segment AB is congruent to segment EF)
